Object Name Date Distance (LD / AU) Velocity (km/s) Est. Diameter Danger
AU
Astronomical Unit — the average Earth-Sun distance (~150 million km). Used to measure distances in the solar system.
LD
Lunar Distance — the average Earth-Moon distance (~384,400 km). More intuitive for close approaches.
v_rel
Relative velocity — how fast the object is moving relative to Earth at closest approach (km/s).
v_inf
Velocity at infinity — the object's speed far from Earth's gravity well. Represents its true speed through space.
Absolute Magnitude (H)
A measure of an asteroid's brightness, which relates to its size. Lower H = larger or brighter object.
Close Approach Date
The date and time when the object passes nearest to Earth.
Diameter
Estimated physical size of the object in meters. Many objects don't have confirmed diameters — these are estimates from brightness.
Danger Tier
A simplified public-communication label based on approach distance only. Does not reflect official NASA Planetary Defense risk assessments.
